Character Posing

For this project of portraying character expressions, I worked to familiarise myself with moving 3D rigs to convey convincing emotions. The purpose of this was to home in on how a character’s face and body would look when they feel a specific emotion, and how I would make that visually interesting to the viewer.

The muscles in the human face can create hundreds of different expressions. In the series of renders I created, I labelled them with a specific emotion and a made-up scenario to go along with it. I used the lighting and colour of the background in the renders to further enhance the emotion I was conveying.

During the planning stage of creating these renders, I familiarised myself with the anatomy of the human face. I did my research with guides on how to draw human emotion. I drew my own references for expression through eyes and mouth to grasp the concepts I’d learnt. I then created five different mood boards conveying the emotions that I wanted to portray. Based on these mood boards I sketched out what my final expressions would look like on the rig.



After the planning stage came to putting the expressions together. Using a royalty-free rig, I used the program Maya to move the facial controls and joint controls and created five different expressions. I came up with scenarios in my head to make the expressions more specific and believable. Once the expressions looked how I wanted them to, I rendered them out as high quality PNGs with a transparent background, having adjusted the scene lighting to reflect the emotion of the character.

Based on the emotion and lighting, I took the renders into Photoshop and added backgrounds to accurately reflect that. I then added text next to the render explaining what the emotion was, and the scenario to go along with it. If some didn’t turn out how I wanted them to, I kept these fails in order to get an idea of what I needed to improve on (for example, rendering out a more appealing silhouette).
