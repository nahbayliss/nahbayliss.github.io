Snow Impressions
Technical Art

In my playable Unity build “Snow Impressions”, I created something where the player can drive a car around on a snowy plane, and the wheels will leave tracks behind in the snow. This was created using Unity’s free assets, and myself coding in C#.

With the use of arrow keys, as you drive your tyre marks will leave imprints in the snow. To add to the atmosphere there is falling snow. As the snow continues to fall, it slowly builds up to cover your tracks. This reduces the lag on the program and makes it easier for your computer to run. I was inspired in this project by the deformation of snow in games such as Red Dead Redemption 2 and Rise of the Tomb Raider. The purpose of this project was for me to create art in unity with technical skills, such as coding.

I already had some base knowledge of coding in C# when beginning this project, but relied on the help of sources on the internet, such as YouTube to guide me in creating this technical art. I created various C# scripts that applied both to the wheels of the player’s car and the snowy plane. I applied physical colliders to the wheels of the car, meaning that the snowy plane could detect when the wheels were touching it. The snowy plane script created deformations at the collision of the wheels, and gradually filled back up again over time to no deformations. The falling snow that the player can see is a particle effect to help simulate that the deformations are really filling back up with snow.
