Architectual
Visualisation

In these high-quality renders of two buildings, I created both traditional and modern architecture. This was created in Maya through 3D sculpting and texturing.

With the help of references and YouTube tutorials, this project helped me expand my knowledge of complex realistic 3D modelling and texturing. I also learnt about making surfaces more realistic, such as how to make a window look like real reflective glass in rendering. The background used in the render is a royalty free 360 degree image.

When creating these buildings, I found that the traditional brick building was easier to create. This was due to its symmetry and geometric shapes. The process of creating the brick building started by creating the walls and windows for the first floor and duplicating it to create the above floors. Then came the creation of the door. For the modern skyscraper, I free-handed some of the asymmetrical aspects of the mesh to add visual interest. The experience of creating these buildings taught me about UV unwrapping complex objects. UV unwrapping is the process of representing the object on a flat mesh so that it can then be textured at an optimised scale. For the modern building, it didn’t require custom textures and instead I created coloured materials and altered the settings to represent different surfaces (such as glass and metal).
